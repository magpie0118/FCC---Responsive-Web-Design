Bessie Coleman

The First Female African American Airplane Pilot


Bessie Coleman next to a Nieuport Type 82, a 27-foot-long biplane with a 40-foot wingspan.

    -1892 Born in Atlanta, Texas
    -1915 Bessie moved to Chicago, IL to live with her brothers and heard many war stories about brave pilots. This inspired her to work two jobs to save up money to become a pilot.
    -1920 Bessie moved to France to work on her Pilot License.
    -1921 She completed her Pilot License and is the first African American and Native American Pilot to earn an international license. She is the first African American woman to earn a pilot license. -1921 She returned to the United States, where she gained a lot of media attention for her accomplishments.
    -1922 She made her first appearance at an Airshow at Curtis Field near New York City.
    -1923 Bessie gained a reputation for being a bold and fearless pilot. She broke a leg and three ribs when her airplane stalled in attempts to complete a risky stunt.
